<p><ul><li>Biomaterials</li><li>Metallic Implants</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Interlocking nails are categorized primarily into two market types: biomaterials and metallic implants. Biomaterials are designed to be biocompatible, often made from polymers or bioactive ceramics, promoting enhanced healing and integration with bone tissue. In contrast, metallic implants, typically crafted from stainless steel or titanium, offer robust mechanical stability and durability for fracture fixation. Both types serve critical roles in orthopedic surgeries, with selection dependent on patient needs, the type of fracture, and desired healing outcomes.</p></p>
