<p><p>The optical biosensor market can be categorized into Type 1 and Type 2 segments. Type 1 biosensors typically involve direct detection techniques that utilize light absorption or fluorescence to identify biological elements, often seen in medical diagnostics and environmental monitoring. Type 2 biosensors, on the other hand, employ more complex methods like surface plasmon resonance or waveguide-based techniques, focusing on high sensitivity and specificity. Both types cater to diverse applications, driving innovation in healthcare, food safety, and biotechnology.</p></p>
